# 📊 Analysis of Drug Overdose Death Rates in the United States (1999–2018)

This project explores national trends in drug overdose deaths across the U.S., using statistical modeling, regression analysis, and visualization across Python, SQL, and R platforms. It reveals key demographic patterns and the rising influence of synthetic opioids like fentanyl.

---

## 🎯 Objective

To analyze drug overdose death trends from 1999 to 2018 and examine disparities based on age, gender, race, and drug type using real-world data from the U.S. Department of Health & Human Services.

## 📈 Key Insights

- Drug overdose deaths have surged steadily since 1999, with synthetic opioids (like fentanyl) causing the largest rise.
- The 25–34 age group is the most affected demographic.
- Racial and gender disparities are evident in overdose rates.
- Regression models (linear and quadratic) helped visualize long-term patterns and predict trends.

---

## 📌 Recommendations

- Improve real-time overdose tracking using tech-enabled systems.
- Target high-risk demographics with education and treatment access.
- Reform prescription and policy controls over synthetic opioids.
